# 2016-USA-Election-Spatial-Analysis
The election victory for Donald J. Trump in 2016 came at rather a surprise, as all forecasting has 
predicted him to lose the election, that his opponent Hillary Clinton to gain an overwhelming victory 
over him, which this prediction was then overturned completely by the actual result of the election. The 
purpose of this analysis is to identify the factors that influenced the American voter’s decision which
brought them to vote for Donald J. Trump in the election in 2016. Here we wish to examine the 
following, that we question if what are the important demographic factors that was causing a strong 
spatial pattern in Trump support? Or was there actually little spatial variation with that the Trump 
voters being evenly distributed in Wisconsin state? Is Trumpism mainly an urban or a rural 
phenomenon? Does it rather have a relationship with being a racial phenomenon?
